Bio

For 19 years I have devoted my career to putting smiles on people's faces. Whether it was the glow of a new bride-to-be, the sentimental tears brought about by a grandmother's refurbished heirloom or simply something new just because, I have touched many lives with jewelry. Now I wish to have an impact on a greater scope of the world. Not everybody needs jewelry but in this day and age everybody needs the internet.

How do the skills I've honed over the years translate to web design?

  • Attention to Detail

    Attention to detail is key, whether it involves setting one hundred 1mm diamonds or looking for that elusive syntax error

  • Plan of Action

    Every ring repair, every 3 carat diamond to be mounted and every website needs a plan of action before beginning to prevent unforeseen complications

  • Client Interaction

    Client interaction is important when determining their wants and needs, including gentle guidance and expert influence to create what will benefit them most

  • Organization & Time Management

    A jewelry shop is often a high-paced, task-oriented, and time-driven environment that benefits greatly from organization and time management, skills that are also valuable to keep a website build on track

  • Personal Standards

    High personal standards translate to only the best for the client

  • Love to Build

    Building something from little more than a thought elicits great pleasure, whether it's the tangible efforts of metal forming or the visual blossoming of a beautifully designed website
